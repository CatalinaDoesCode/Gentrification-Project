What is Gentrification?

Gentrification is when wealthy people come into low income neighborhoods. This usually leads to the development of expensive housing and buisnesses.

Mission Statement

Gentrification is an issue that deeply impacts people of color in urban areas. It is an extremely complex issue with multiple factors. This site should serve as a launching pad for further learning.

Facts

  • Gentrification affects people who live in low income neighborhoods. These people tend to be people of color.
  • The problem is not individuals. It is usually large real estate companies buying real estate in "up and coming" neighborhoods.
  • The cost of living goes up because the area now has a higher value. Buisnesses and real estate companies value profit so they increase prices of goods and rent also goes up.
  • As a result the poor people who once lived in the gentrifying area can no longer afford to live there. These people then become displaced in order to appease the affluent.
  • Manufacturing jobs are lost to gentrification. Due to increasing rent prices small manufacturers can't afford to stay in once convenient locations.
  • Service jobs (retail and food services) do increase in gentrifying areas. New buisnesses = more jobs
